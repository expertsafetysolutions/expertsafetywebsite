Choosing the wrong extinguisher for a given fire class doesn't just fail to
put the fire out — in the case of water on an electrical fire, it can actively
make things worse. This guide walks through how to match extinguisher type to
the fire risks actually present on an industrial site.

## Start with your fire classes

Most industrial sites carry a mix of Class A (ordinary combustibles like wood,
paper and packaging), Class B (flammable liquids — fuels, solvents, paints)
and electrical fire risk around panels, motors and control rooms. A few also
carry Class F risk in kitchens or canteens.

## Matching extinguisher type to risk

**ABC dry powder** extinguishers cover Class A, B and C in one unit, which is
why they're the default choice for general factory floors and warehouse
aisles — see our [ABC Dry Powder Extinguisher](/products/fire-extinguishers/abc-dry-powder-extinguisher-6kg).

**CO₂ extinguishers** leave no residue, making them the right call for server
rooms, switchgear and control panels where powder would damage equipment —
see our [CO₂ Fire Extinguisher](/products/fire-extinguishers/co2-fire-extinguisher-4-5kg).

**Foam (AFFF) extinguishers** blanket flammable liquid fires and prevent
reignition, suited to fuel stores, paint stores and generator rooms.

**Water mist extinguishers** are a newer, versatile option safe for use
around people and electrical equipment, increasingly specified for offices
and healthcare settings.

## Coverage and placement

Beyond selecting the right type, extinguisher count and placement need to
match your floor area and travel distance requirements under IS 2190. A
[fire risk assessment](/services/fire-risk-assessment) will map out exact
coverage gaps rather than relying on a rule of thumb.
