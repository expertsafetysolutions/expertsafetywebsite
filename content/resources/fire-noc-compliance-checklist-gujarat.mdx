Fire No-Objection Certificate (NOC) inspections check both the physical
condition of your fire safety equipment and the documentation behind it.
This checklist covers the areas we most commonly see flagged during
inspections of factories and warehouses in Gujarat.

## Equipment checks

- Extinguishers present in required numbers, in-date and tagged with the
  last service date
- Hydrant system pressure-tested and landing valves functional
- Fire alarm panel operational with no outstanding fault indications
- Emergency and exit signage installed, visible and undamaged
- Escape routes and assembly points clear of obstruction

## Documentation checks

- Extinguisher and hydrant test/refill records for the current cycle
- Fire alarm system test and maintenance log
- Staff fire safety training records and attendance certificates
- A current fire risk assessment report
- Evacuation drill records for the reporting period

## Where sites commonly fall short

The most frequent gaps we find during a [fire risk assessment](/services/fire-risk-assessment)
aren't missing equipment — they're missing paperwork: extinguishers that were
serviced but the tag wasn't updated, or a training session that happened but
was never logged. Keeping a single, current compliance file resolves most of
this.
